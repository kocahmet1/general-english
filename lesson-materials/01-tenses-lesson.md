#### ğŸ”µ PAST SIMPLE
| Component | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| **Affirmative** | Subject + V2 (past form) |

**Examples:**
- I **visited** Paris last summer. âœ“
- She **didn't call** me yesterday. âœ“

#### ğŸ”µ PAST CONTINUOUS
| Component | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| **Affirmative** | Subject + was/were + V-ing |
| **Negative** | Subject + was/were + not + V-ing |
| **Question** | Was/Were + subject + V-ing? |

**Examples:**
- I **was reading** when she arrived. âœ“

#### ğŸ”µ PAST PERFECT
| Component | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| **Affirmative** | Subject + had + V3 (past participle) |
| **Negative** | Subject + had not + V3 |
| **Question** | Had + subject + V3? |

**Signal Words:** `before`, `after`, `already`, `by the time`, `when`, `never... until`

**Examples:**

#### Story B: "The Journey"

### Exercise 1: ANSWER KEY

#### Story A: "The Interview"
- Yesterday, I **went** (go) to a job interview.
- Before I **arrived** (arrive), I **had prepared** (prepare) for three days.
- When the interviewer asked me about my experience, I told her that I **had worked** (work) in marketing for five years.
- I **haven't heard** (not/hear) back from them yet.
- I **have been waiting** (wait) for their call since last week.
- Tomorrow, I **will call** (call) them if I **haven't received / don't receive** (not/receive) any news by then.

#### Story B: "The Journey"
- Last summer, my family **decided** (decide) to go on a road trip.
- We **had never traveled** (never/travel) across the country before, so we were very excited.
- My father **had driven / drove** (drive) for ten hours before we **reached** (reach) our first destination.
- Right now, I **am looking** (look) at the photos from that trip.
- I **have already shown** (already/show) them to all my friends.
- Next summer, we **are planning / plan** (plan) to visit a new country.

#### Story C: "The Project"
- I **have been working** (work) on this project since Monday.
- Before I **started** (start), I **had read** (read) all the instructions carefully.
- Yesterday, I **finished** (finish) the first section, but I **haven't completed** (not/complete) the second one yet.
- My boss **asks / has been asking** (ask) me about my progress every day.
- Right now, I **am trying** (try) to solve a difficult problem.
- I hope I **will finish / will have finished** (finish) everything by Friday.

### When to Use Each Tense:

| Tense | Use When... | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Past Simple | Action finished in the past | *I ate pizza yesterday.* |
| Past Continuous | Action in progress at past time | *I was eating when you called.* |
| Past Perfect | Action before another past action | *I had eaten before you came.* |
| Present Simple | Habits, facts, routines | *I eat breakfast every day.* |
| Present Continuous | Happening now / temporary | *I am eating right now.* |
| Present Perfect | Past â†’ Now connection | *I have eaten sushi before.* |
| Future Simple | Predictions, promises | *I will eat later.* |
| Future Continuous | In progress at future time | *I will be eating at 7 PM.* |
