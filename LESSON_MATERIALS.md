# Teacher's Lesson Guide & Material Bank

This document organizes the content available in the English Learning App into a structure suitable for bi-weekly lessons. It focuses on the student's weak areas: **Tenses, Prepositions, Vocabulary, and Phrases**.

## Lesson Structure Proposal (60 Minutes)

1.  **Warm-up / Grammar Repair (10-15 mins)**: Targeted drills on weak areas.
2.  **Speaking & Fluency (20-25 mins)**: Structured conversation practice.
3.  **Contextual Practice (15-20 mins)**: Reading or Role-play for vocabulary in context.
4.  **Wrap-up (5 mins)**: Review of errors and homework assignment.

## Part 1: Grammar Repair Materials
*Source: `src/data/sampleExam.ts`*

Use these questions not just as a quiz, but as discussion points. Ask: *"Why is B correct?"* or *"Why is A wrong?"*

### Focus: Tenses (Past, Present, Perfect)
*Selected questions to diagnose and fix tense confusion.*

*   **Starter/Elementary:**
    *   "He \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ go to work yesterday." (Did vs Do) - *Q10*
    *   "I \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ coffee every morning." (Present Simple) - *Q6*
    *   "Have you ever \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ to Paris?" (Present Perfect) - *Q15*
*   **Intermediate:**
    *   "While I \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ down the street, I saw a friend." (Past Continuous) - *Q21*
    *   "By the time we arrived, the train \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_." (Past Perfect) - *Q32*
    *   "If it rains, we \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ inside." (Conditionals/Future) - *Q23*

### Focus: Prepositions & Phrasal Verbs
*Common weak points for many learners.*

*   **Basics:**
    *   "I usually go to school \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ bus." (by/on/in) - *Q16*
    *   "I have lived here \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ 2010." (since/for) - *Q24*
*   **Advanced/Phrasal:**
    *   "The meeting has been put \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ until tomorrow." (off/on/away) - *Q41*
    *   "She is accused \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ stealing..." (of/with/for) - *Q44*
    *   "We need to cut down \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ our expenses." (on/at/to) - *Q48*

### Focus: Phrases & Collocations
*   "He made a \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ recovery..." (speedy/fast/quick) - *Q66*
*   "I can't \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ the difference..." (tell/say/speak) - *Q69*

## Part 2: Speaking & Conversation Materials
*Source: `src/data/speakingQuestions.ts`*

### Section 1: "Warm-up" Chats (Casual)
*Good for getting the student talking comfortably.*
*   **Work**: "What are your main responsibilities at work?" / "Would you like to change your job?"
*   **Hobbies**: "How much time do you spend on your hobbies?"
*   **Technology**: "Do you think the internet has changed people's lives?"

### Section 2: "Long Turn" (Fluency & Coherence)
*Give the student 1 minute to prepare, then ask them to speak for 1-2 minutes continuously.*
*   **Topic: A Person** - Describe a teacher/person who influenced you.
*   **Topic: A Place** - Describe a city you have visited.
*   **Topic: An Event** - Describe a memorable celebration.

### Section 3: Deep Discussion (Abstract Ideas)
*Best for advanced vocabulary and complex sentence structures.*
*   **Education**: "Do you think online learning is as effective as traditional classroom learning?"
*   **Environment**: "How can businesses become more environmentally responsible?"
*   **Culture**: "How can countries balance modernization with preserving traditions?"

## Part 3: Reading & Contextual Vocabulary
*Source: `src/data/readingPassages.ts`*

Use these passages to see vocabulary in action.

1.  **"The History of Coffee" (Intermediate)**
    *   *Theme*: History, Culture.
    *   *Key Vocab*: *Cultivated, establishments, resistance, consumption, stimulant*.
    *   *Activity*: Have student read paragraph 3 aloud to check pronunciation of past tense verbs (arrived, gained, called).

2.  **"The Rise of Remote Work" (Intermediate)**
    *   *Theme*: Business, Modern Life.
    *   *Key Vocab*: *Accelerated, productivity, isolation, blurred, hybrid*.
    *   *Activity*: Ask student to summarize the "Pros and Cons" mentioned in the text.

3.  **"Climate Change and Ocean Ecosystems" (Upper Intermediate)**
    *   *Theme*: Science, Environment.
    *   *Key Vocab*: *Unprecedented, symbiotic, extinction, resilient, restoration*.

## Part 4: Role Play (Real-life Practice)
*Source: `src/data/listeningQuestions.ts` (Transcripts)*

Use the transcripts as scripts. You take one role, the student takes the other.

1.  **Hotel Reservation**
    *   *Roles*: Receptionist vs. Caller.
    *   *Focus*: Dates, numbers, polite requests ("Could we have...", "I'd like to...").
2.  **Medical Appointment**
    *   *Roles*: Receptionist vs. Patient.
    *   *Focus*: Describing symptoms ("They come and go", "Getting worse"), scheduling times.
3.  **University Discussion** (Advanced)
    *   *Roles*: Tutor vs. Student.
    *   *Focus*: Academic language, discussing plans and progress.
